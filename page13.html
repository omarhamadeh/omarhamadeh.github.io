"Optimistic Doubt"
Sometimes, we tend to retaliate ourselves amidst our peak. The reason for this perpetual happening is an irrational fear of change. One of my favorite musicians once said: “through other people, we become ourselves”. What this quote essentially describes is: the advantage of multi-dimensionality. To put it simply, metamorphosis. Personally, I had always been fond of the art of theatre, and the nature of performative art. I believe this background passion has enabled me to explore the different slots of identities that were given to me, psycho-spiritually. Even though, I have not experienced the “become ourselves” part of the quote yet, because I have yet to understand whether “myself” is a consciously fluid and unknown entity or whether it is something of which I have yet to conclude. Multiple attempts at research psychology have alerted me with Dissociative Identity Disorder symptoms, but yet, nothing was I diagnosed of. After my diagnosis (or actually, lack thereof), I have understood that whatever this shapeshifting universe I reside in is, it is something that prevents me from Jean-Paul Sartre’s concept of ‘Bad Faith’, and the dangers of making a definite, monolithic conclusion of lifestyle theory or desire. It is now my home, of optimistic doubt.
