"WEAK"
Sometimes we tend to retaliate, involuntarily, as a reaction to our loss. Eventhough this might create a venomous ripple that spreads into our lovers' or family members' psyche; but this ripple is a quintessential and natural consequence of pain. The only mechanism for which wounds can be healed is if they are given a chance to bleed. Water yourself. The problem is that everybody hurts, but nobody wants to bleed. A fearful subjugation it is, to this volcanic red substance, that erupts out of us, convicted by our species' "aggressive nature". But why is it outlandish to vomit onto each other the crippling anxiety that has been trapped inside of us for a lifetime? Why can't we create a medium for our pain for which it can be expressed shamelessly and organically? Why is vulnerability perceived as a weakness? Would you love me better if my body was dripping with blood, on your doorstep? Or if I'd perhaps wore an armor suit?
